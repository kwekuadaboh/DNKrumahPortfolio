My MBA Dissertation was on Credit Risk and Profitability of Rural Banks in Ghana and a summary of my research methods, findings and recommendation are show below:

The main objective study was to examine the relationship between credit risk and profitability of rural banks in Ghana. The motivating question is, if commercial banks with larger capital reserve and capital adequacy are even at risk to the credit risks then how much more that of rural banks.
The descriptive research design was used for this particular study. This design was chosen because it may be either be time variant or invariant. That is flexible with regard to its approach (quantitative and qualitative). The choice of this design is informed by the view of Creswell, Clark, Gutmann and Hanson (2015) that this design is flexible and can even incorporate causal or experimental studies. Data was sourced from the Microfinance Information Exchange (MIX)dataset. A dataset housed by the World Bank on microfinance institutions and rural banks in the world.
In the descriptive statistics, almost all the variables were seen to be normally distributed. Before the main model some diagnostic tests conducted such as the heteroscedasticity and tolerance (VIF) were all passed. The Hausman selection model preferred the fixed effect model over the random effect model. All the variables were statistically significant and while NPL, and LLR were negatively related to profitability, (CAR) and size positively affect the banksâ€™ profitability. The study also had enough evidence to reject all the study hypotheses that no relationship exists between credit risk variables (CAR, NPL, LLR) and rural banks profitability.
It can be concluded that credit risks are very instrumental to the profitability of a bank. This conclusion is premised on the basis that all the variables in the study measuring credit risk were significant and followed the expected signs per the study. Thus for banks to be profitable they should give close consideration to the health of the capital and asset, they should also be mindful of how loans are granted and above all be mindful on the risk they put themselves through.
  • Banks are advised to have up their capital adequacy since it provides cushion against losses and also boosts the confidence of the depositors, public and the regulatory authority to do more business.
  • Banks must also be wary on the rate at which credits are lost to either inefficiencies or investments. This is because the higher the rates, the rate the lower their confidence in business and profit margins.
  • Furthermore, banks should find ways of growing deposits or savings. This is because it increases their assets and dominance or size in doing business. It also helps the banks have access to more cash for investments.
